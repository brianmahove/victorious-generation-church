Prophet Kudakwashe Jose

Zimbabwean born, Prophet Kudakwashe Jose was called into ministry at the tender age of 17. That journey began with a call to seek the Lord alone on the mountains of Zimbiru, Zimbabwe, where he ended up living for three years. The Lord supernaturally provided for his daily needs and trained him on that mountain until he was ordained into ministry from 2017.

Prophet Kudakwashe Jose is used mightily by God in powerful miracles, signs and wonders. The blind see, the lame walk and the dead are raised — broken lives are restored and the good news is proclaimed to the poor (Matthew 11:5).

As a man of compassion for God's people, Prophet Kudakwashe Jose is found in outreach ministries in the small towns and villages all over Zimbabwe or in praying for the sick and tormented in his free one-on-one sessions every Tuesday (for all) and Wednesday (partners only).

Serving the Lord Jesus Christ together with his lovely wife, Prophetess Letwin Jose, and their two children, the Lord continues to add to the church daily those who are being saved (Acts 2:47).

Who We Are

Our Mission & Purpose

Our Mission

The Church's primary aim and objective is to practice and propagate what our Lord Jesus Christ commanded in Mark 16:15-16 — through the provision of a place of worship, prayer, deliverance, healing and winning of souls to Christ.

"Go into all the world and preach the gospel to all creation." — Mark 16:15

Our Purpose

  • Winning souls for God's Kingdom by every method permissible under the Word of God.
  • Ministering to the spiritual, intellectual, physical, emotional and economic needs of all people through spreading the gospel of Christ.
  • Recruiting members and aligning them for the second coming of the Lord Jesus Christ.
  • Establishing platforms and centres for the purpose of fellowship of members.
  • Setting up governance structures in accordance with the Church's Constitution.
  • To train and capacitate pastors for the spread of well-drilled and accurate doctrine.

Reference scriptures: Mark 16:15–18 · Matthew 28:18–20
